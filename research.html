The Cyber Identity and Behavior Research (CIBeR) Lab focuses on research pertaining to Identity and Access Management, with a particular emphasis on mobile biometrics and inclusive user authentication systems. We also specialize in Smart Sensing, particularly for analyzing human behavior and exploring interdisciplinary applications of behavior tracking. Additionally, we engage in research within the field of natural language processing (NLP), which can be explored further at the [NLP Group](https://nlp-grp.github.io/) at USF. CIBeR is under the direction of Dr. Tempestt Neal, an assistant professor in the Department of Computer Science and Engineering at the University of South Florida.
